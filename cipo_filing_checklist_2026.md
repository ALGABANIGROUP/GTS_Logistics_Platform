# CIPO Filing Checklist

## Scope
This checklist is for filing a Canadian patent application through CIPO, not a U.S.-style provisional application.

## Confirm Before Filing
- Confirm inventorship.
- Confirm applicant entitlement.
- Confirm that the applicant name and address are exactly as intended for filing.
- Confirm small entity status under the current Canadian definition before paying reduced fees.
- Confirm the specification is technically accurate and does not overstate unimplemented or unsupported embodiments.
- Confirm the abstract is 150 words or fewer.
- Confirm the claim count is 20 or fewer if you want to avoid excess-claim fees at filing and examination.

## MyCIPO Filing Inputs
- Title of invention
- Applicant name and address
- Inventor name and address
- Statement of entitlement
- Priority claim details, if any
- Patent agent information, if any

## Filing-Date Minimum
To secure a filing date, CIPO states that the submission must at minimum identify that a patent is sought, identify the applicant, provide contact information, and include something that appears to be a description of the invention. A compliant abstract is required for a complete application, but not for obtaining the initial filing date.

## Fees To Recheck At Submission Time
- Application fee, small entity: CAD 241.24
- Application fee, standard: CAD 595.06
- Examination fee, small entity: CAD 490.68
- Examination fee, standard: CAD 1,210.36
- Excess claim fee above 20 claims: CAD 59.97 per claim for small entity, CAD 119.94 per claim for standard entity

## Timing Notes
- Examination must be requested within 4 years from the Canadian filing date.
- In most cases, publication occurs 18 months after the earliest priority date or filing date.
- CIPO guidance indicates a filing certificate is generally sent within about 15 business days after a compliant filing is received.

## Recommended Final Review Items
- Replace any placeholders in the declaration.
- Convert the specification into your preferred filing format, typically PDF.
- Prepare formal drawings with figure numbering matching the specification.
- Consider reviewing the claims for unity and claim scope with a Canadian patent agent before filing.
